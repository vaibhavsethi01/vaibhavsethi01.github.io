    ★ Flagship

    Mortgage Stress-Testing Engine

    A loan-level probability-of-default model on 350K+ real Freddie Mac mortgages, projected under the Federal Reserve's 2026 recession scenarios — the CCAR/DFAST exercise banks run for their regulator.

    Engineered 19M loan-month records in DuckDB; trained a calibrated PD model (logistic benchmark + XGBoost challenger) validated out-of-time by vintage, then fit a discrete-time hazard with macroeconomic covariates and rolled the portfolio forward 13 quarters under the Fed's baseline vs severely-adverse paths. Reproduced in SAS (PROC LOGISTIC) and R (glm), with SHAP reason codes, a model card for governance, and a live web dashboard + stress simulator.

    Banking Risk Platform

    A full credit-scoring and expected-loss system, built end to end in R.

    PD model training with XGBoost, benchmarked against logistic regression under severe class imbalance with stratified k-fold CV; evaluated on ROC AUC and PR AUC rather than accuracy, and calibrated with Platt scaling so outputs are usable as proper PD estimates. Portfolio-level expected loss via PD × LGD × EAD, per-prediction SHAP reason codes, and a model card shipped for governance review.

    LIGO Gravitational-Wave Glitch Classifier

    Separating real astrophysical signal from instrument noise — literally.

    Multi-class classification of 7,966 detector-noise samples across 22 severely imbalanced glitch classes (67× ratio between largest and smallest). Tuned Random Forest via grid search and 3-fold CV: 88% accuracy, 0.81 macro F1, with CV and test scores within 0.01 — no overfit. Feature importance matched the physics of how glitches separate in time-frequency space.

    The candidate

    I'm a fourth-year UBC student finishing a combined major in Statistics and Physics. Most statistics students stop at the notebook — a model that scores well on a held-out set and never leaves the laptop. I'm interested in the part after that: is the probability calibrated, can I explain every score, and do I know where it breaks?

    That instinct comes from the physics half of the degree. Telling a real gravitational-wave event from detector noise is the same discipline as telling a real default signal from a noisy credit file — you respect the noise, you quantify your uncertainty, and you don't trust a number you can't defend. It's why I evaluate on PR AUC instead of accuracy when defaults are rare, and why every model I build ships with a model card.
