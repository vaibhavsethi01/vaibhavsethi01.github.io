Why this project

Banks subject to the Dodd-Frank Act Stress Test (DFAST) must show their regulator how their loan book would perform under a hypothetical severe recession. This project reproduces that workflow end-to-end on real public mortgage data: build a calibrated PD model, link it to macroeconomic drivers, and roll the book forward quarter-by-quarter under the Fed's published 2026 scenarios — reporting the result in dollars and loss-rate, the way a risk committee reads it.

How it works

  1. Data engineering (DuckDB). 350K real Freddie Mac loans, 19M loan-month records queried directly in DuckDB. Default = 180+ DPD or credit-event; prepayment is a competing risk.
  2. PD model. Logistic benchmark + XGBoost challenger, validated out-of-time by vintage, scored on Gini/KS/PR-AUC, isotonic-calibrated, explained with SHAP.
  3. Macro linkage. A discrete-time hazard where the per-quarter default hazard depends on loan age, features, unemployment, and a mark-to-market LTV (the house-price channel).
  4. Stress projection. Roll the hazard forward 13 quarters under the Fed's baseline vs severely-adverse macro paths, accounting for competing prepayment.
  5. LGD & Expected Loss. EL = PD × LGD × EAD, aggregated per scenario into the headline loss numbers.
