Business impact: at the chosen threshold, expected loss rate on accepted customers is ~5.94% (empirical, test). On a hypothetical $1B portfolio this implies material avoided losses (order-of-magnitude: tens of millions USD annually) versus an unmodeled baseline.

2 · What I built

A production-oriented credit-risk pipeline in R using tidymodels + xgboost: robust preprocessing (median imputation, Yeo-Johnson transform, normalization), SMOTE for class imbalance, hyperparameter tuning, Platt calibration, and explainability (VIP + SHAP sampling).

3 · Data

  • Source: UCI — Default of Credit Card Clients (Taiwan)
  • Rows: 30,000
  • Predictors: LIMIT_BAL, SEX, EDUCATION, MARRIAGE, AGE, PAY_0..PAY_6, BILL_AMT1..3, PAY_AMT1..3, derived DTI
  • Target: default_payment_next_month (0 / 1)

4 · Pipeline & modeling choices

  • Train / calibration / test splits: 80% / 8% / 12%, stratified, reproducible seed
  • Recipe: drop technical columns, median impute, zero-variance filter, Yeo-Johnson → normalize, one-hot encode categoricals
  • Resampling: 5-fold CV for tuning
  • Models compared: Logistic Regression (GLM) benchmark vs XGBoost challenger
  • Calibration: Platt scaling on a holdout calibration set
  • Threshold: business-aware — maximize KS/Youden subject to expected-loss-rate constraint (ELR ≤ 6%) on the accepted population

6 · Explainability & checks

  • Global importance (VIP): top features — PAY_0 (recent payment status), LIMIT_BAL, PAY_2, PAY_3, AGE, PAY_6
  • Local explainability: SHAP-style explanations on a 500-sample subset; top contributors exported for review
  • Calibration: decile reliability plot; Platt scaling aligns predicted PDs to observed default frequencies
  • Stability: train vs test comparison + monitoring hooks for PD and feature-distribution drift

7 · Fairness & risk management

Subgroup analysis by SEX, EDUCATION, and AGE buckets found no alarming disparate impact on the training/test data. The model is not production-approved until broader external validation and business policy sign-off. Governance artifacts (workflow, calibrator, metrics) are versioned in the repo; monitoring plan: quarterly calibration check, annual retrain (or on drift).

8 · Reproducibility

Key artifacts: models/final_xgb_workflow.rds, models/platt_scaler.rds, docs/metrics_summary.csv, docs/Model-Card.html, analysis/step5_evaluation.R.

Business-case figures are illustrative; real deployment requires vintage analysis, migration matrices, macro overlays, and provisioning practices.
